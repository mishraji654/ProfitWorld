Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between stocks and bonds?

Stocks represent ownership in a company, providing potential for capital appreciation and dividends. Bonds, on the other hand, are debt instruments issued by governments or corporations, offering fixed interest payments over time.

How does trading differ from investing?

Trading involves buying and selling financial instruments frequently within shorter timeframes, aiming to profit from market fluctuations. Investing, meanwhile, focuses on buying assets with the intent of holding them for the long term to build wealth through capital appreciation and income generation.

What are the key factors influencing stock prices?

Stock prices are influenced by various factors, including company earnings reports, economic indicators, geopolitical events, interest rates, investor sentiment, and industry trends.

What is risk management in trading?

Risk management in trading involves strategies to minimize potential losses and protect capital. This includes setting stop-loss orders, diversifying investments, avoiding excessive leverage, and conducting thorough research before making trading decisions.

How do I choose a trading strategy?

Selecting a trading strategy depends on factors such as risk tolerance, investment goals, time commitment, and market expertise. Common strategies include day trading, swing trading, value investing, momentum trading, and options trading, each with its own risk-reward profile.

What are the costs associated with trading?

Trading costs may include brokerage fees, commissions, spreads (for forex trading), exchange fees, and taxes. It's essential to understand these costs upfront to assess their impact on trading profitability.
